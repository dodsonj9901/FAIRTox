Zacharewski Lab Mission Statement

Exposure to environmental chemicals, drugs, and natural products can result in toxicity to humans and wildlife. Our lab is interested in elucidating the underlying mechanisms of nuclear receptor-mediated toxicity in order to better understand human risk. Our approach involves the integration of data from in vitro and in vivo studies with computational (bioinformatics) techniques to further elucidate the physiological, cellular, and molecular effects associated with a toxic response within the context of a whole organism, its genome, proteome, and metabolome.

About FAIRTox

This application serves as an interface between the data generated by our lab and the user. More specifically, it aims to broaden the accessibility of gene expression data to allow for more efficient analysis. The inspiration for this project was the National Institute of Health's initiative to promote 'FAIR' data. FAIR strives for data that is findable, accessible, interoperable, and reusable. We believe that this application meets the requirements of FAIR and sets a precedent for responsible data management.
